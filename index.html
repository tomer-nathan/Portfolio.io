Intro

I have always been passionate about problem-solving, analytical thinking, and continuous learning. From a young age, I sought opportunities to challenge myself and grow both personally and professionally.

At 18, I took on the rigorous selection process for Unit 8200, one of the IDF’s most prestigious intelligence units, and was accepted as a Data Analyst. After an intensive 5.5-month training program, I assumed a role where I worked with complex data, conducted in-depth analyses, and provided insights that supported critical decision-making processes. This experience honed my ability to work under pressure, think strategically, and leverage data-driven solutions.

Following my military service, I decided to deepen my expertise in data analysis and enrolled in a professional program at John Bryce in Tel Aviv. Throughout my studies, I have transitioned from working with proprietary military technologies to mastering civilian tools, gaining hands-on experience in SQL, Python, Power BI, Excel, and AI-driven analytics.

Now, I am eager to apply my analytical skills, technical expertise, and problem-solving mindset in a professional setting, contributing value through data-driven insights and impactful decision-making.

About

During my military service as a Data Analyst in Unit 8200, I was responsible for analyzing complex data and delivering real-time insights to support high-stakes decision-making.

In my final year of service, as war broke out, my role became even more critical, requiring me to operate under intense pressure where accuracy, efficiency, and rapid judgment were crucial. I developed expertise in real-time data analysis, problem-solving under tight deadlines, and communicating critical insights to senior decision-makers. These experiences strengthened my ability to work under extreme conditions, think analytically, and translate data into actionable intelligence in fast-paced, high-impact environments.
